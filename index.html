Kids Who Rose.
Real stories from real kids proving that disability is never the end of the story.

🌟
Oliver
TOURETTE SYNDROME · AGE 10
"I have Tourette syndrome and I still built 8 real products, a social network, a game studio, and a company — all before middle school. My tics don't stop me. They remind me I'm built different. Disability doesn't hold you back. It makes you fight harder."
"
💙
Maya
AUTISM SPECTRUM · AGE 12
"People said I'd never make real friends because of my autism. But I wrote a book about my experiences and now kids all over the world message me saying it changed their life. I'm not weird. I just see the world differently — and that's a gift."
"
💚
Jordan
CEREBRAL PALSY · AGE 11
"Everyone at school stared at my wheelchair like it was a big deal. So I turned it into a go-kart and raced it around the gym during recess. Now everybody wants a ride. Don't hide what makes you different — make it awesome."
"
💜
Zara
ADHD + DYSLEXIA · AGE 13
"My teachers said I couldn't read well enough to write stories. I have dyslexia AND ADHD. So I started making videos instead — and now I have 40,000 subscribers. My brain works at 100mph and that's not a problem. That's a superpower."
"
❤️
Eli
DEAF · AGE 9
"I can't hear music, but I learned to feel the vibrations and now I make beats on my phone. My whole class asks me to DJ the school dance. Being deaf didn't take music from me. It gave me a way to experience it that no one else can."
